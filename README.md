# SENN-Framework  
## Space Energy Node Network (SENN)

---

## Overview

SENN (Space Energy Node Network) is a feasibility-oriented framework for a distributed space-based energy infrastructure.

It shifts space propulsion from:
> **onboard fuel dependence → network-based energy relay**

Instead of each spacecraft carrying its own energy for the entire mission, SENN proposes a system of **energy nodes** that:

- collect energy
- transmit energy
- relay energy across space
- support propulsion and navigation

---

## Core Concept

> Not a propulsion device, but an infrastructure model.

SENN transforms space travel into:

```text
Node → Node → Node

Where spacecraft move through a network of energy support rather than isolated missions.

Key Capabilities
Node-to-node energy transmission (laser / microwave)
Directed energy propulsion support (light sail / receiver systems)
Orbital-based infrastructure scaling
Long-duration mission support without full onboard fuel dependency
Minimum Viable Scope (v1.0)

This repository focuses on:

Earth–Moon system (cislunar space)
Lagrange point deployment
Small-scale energy relay validation
Feasibility-driven engineering constraints
System Philosophy

SENN is based on three principles:

Decentralization
No single energy source dependency
Scalability
Node-based expansion across orbital regions
Feasibility-first design
Grounded in known physics and near-term engineering
