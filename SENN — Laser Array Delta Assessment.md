# SENN — Laser Array Delta Assessment (PUBLIC)

## Overview
This document presents a high-level evaluation of integrating distributed phase-controlled laser arrays into the SENN (Space Energy Node Network) propulsion layer.

The purpose is to assess whether transitioning from a single-source laser model to a multi-node coherent array architecture can meaningfully improve system feasibility.

---

## Core Hypothesis

> Can distributed laser arrays with phase control reshape the optical field sufficiently to improve propulsion efficiency and reduce long-distance beam degradation?

---

## High-Level Conclusion

Classification: Improvement with Limits

Distributed laser arrays provide:

- Improved beam steering capability  
- Enhanced directional control  
- Potential reduction in effective divergence at moderate scales  
- Redundancy across nodes  

However, they do not eliminate fundamental constraints, including:

- Diffraction limits  
- Energy delivery decay over distance  
- System-wide precision requirements  
- Infrastructure complexity  

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Beam Control vs Physical Limits
Phase-controlled arrays allow beam shaping and steering, but cannot bypass the fundamental relationship:

> Beam divergence is governed by wavelength and effective aperture.

---

### 2. Distributed vs Centralized Trade-off
Replacing a centralized system with distributed nodes:

- Reduces single-point failure  
- Introduces synchronization complexity  
- Shifts bottlenecks rather than removing them  

---

### 3. System Role Adjustment
Laser arrays are better positioned as:

- Localized propulsion support systems
- Precision control layers
- Energy relay components

rather than:

- Primary deep-space propulsion drivers

---

## Architectural Reframing

SENN should not be interpreted as a singular propulsion system, but as a multi-layered support architecture, where:

- Laser arrays operate within bounded regions  
- Propulsion is staged and environment-dependent  
- Energy distribution is modular rather than continuous  

---

## Positioning Statement

This document does not claim a resolved propulsion system.

It defines:

> A structured evolution from conceptual architecture to constrained engineering reality.
